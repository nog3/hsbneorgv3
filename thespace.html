217 MacArthur Ave, Eagle Farm, QLD 4009

Our campus includes 2 buildings on a 3,500m2 plot. We have a general work room, a classroom, dedicated rooms for Digital Fabrication and geeky Crafts, the Boneyard full of scavengable parts, as well as dedicated Woodworking and Metal working rooms.

What can you find at HSBNE?

The Green Room

Our clean building has a large common area devoted as a general workspace and hangout zone, and we call this the Green Room.

The Hipster Lounge

The Hipster Lounge is our front entryway and is the first part of the space you'll see after coming through our big blue doors. It contains displays of things made at the space and is a great area to relax in.

The Classroom

The classroom is configurable to allow members to teach anything and is available for external groups to book on request. It has a capacity of around 30 people.

Member Storage

We devote an entire, locked to non-members room for members to temporarily store their tools and projects in 55L tubs.

The Boneyard

HSBNE accepts donations from businesses and our members quite regularly. We store these donations in a room known as the boneyard. If you're a member and you need to scavenge for a switch here or a motor there, the Boneyard is the place to look.

Craft Punk

Craft Punk is home to many crafts, from costuming all the way to sewing and even geekier things. It houses equipment for heat press transfer onto shirts, mugs and plates as well as screen printing gear. Additionally, it is the home for our retro-fitted vacuum former which is a repurposed industrial Vacuum Sealer.

Digital Fabrication

Our Digital Fabrication room houses several useful machines, from our retrofitted chinese Laser Cutters to an A0 plotter and several 3d Printers. Need to prototype something? This is the spot.

Woodworking

Our Woodworking area is rapidly shaping up to be one of the best equipped parts of the space. It is now located in a much larger room and is stocked with general woodworking gear like lathes, drill presses, table saws, thicknessers and planers.

Metalworking

Our best equipped room, metalworking holds 2 lathes, a manual mill, metal bandsaw, linisher, and welding equipment as well as an assortment of finishing tools like grinders and a linisher. Metalworking holds our 2 ton Gantry, block-and-tackle, and our full sized car grease trap. It also is home to our Giganto-Printer, a 5x3m poster printer being converted for a new hackerspace life.

How do I get to HSBNE?

By Car

  • Ideal Driving Route

Make your way onto the ICB Northbound, follow through onto Kingsford Smith Drive. Take a right at Remora Rd, and a left at the first roundabout, onto MacArthur Ave. Continue past the first (and only) left hand turn (Barcham St) and turn right into our driveway just past that.

By Public Transport

  • Translink Planner
  • Walking Route from Northshore Hamilton Ferry

The best City Cat terminal to go to the Northshore Hamilton City Cat terminal. Once you arrive walk up the ramp and through the park, turn left and walk about 600m past the really TALL shed and car park and the space is the first driveway on your left. It's about 700m all up.

By Walking / Riding

Shortest most direct route. 7.9Km This is the shortest quickest way there from the city, lots of traffic, pedestrians and 99% main roads. I would only ride this if you are happy riding on the road in mains road traffic conditions.

Safest cycling route. 10.1Km This route takes you round the back of the city and mostly stays to bike paths or bike lanes, it is a bit hillier but there is a lot less traffic to deal with.

The scenic route. 15.3Km This is a reasonably long and mostly flat ride, there are a few hills when you come round past the story bridge including one evilly steep one, but it's well worth doing. Wear plenty of sunscreen and bring water to drink as there's a few gaps with little cover and no water to be found.
